About

From the age of five, dancing has been the heartbeat of Nishtha Joshi’s life. She started learning Bharatnatyam at the age of five for 3 consecutive years. Later her family moved to Delhi and there she started her Kathak Journey. Trained in the Lucknow gharana, she has completed her Master’s in Kathak and over two decades of immersive practice. Guided by her Guru Shri Dwaipayan Das and enriched through workshops with luminaries like Pandit Birju Maharaj, Vidushi Saswati Sen, and Vidushi Malti Shyam, Nishtha carries forward a lineage steeped in tradition and artistry.

Her performances weave together refined nazakat, delicate abhinaya, and intricate footwork, breathing life into both timeless compositions and contemporary choreographies.

Dedicated to preserving the purity of the Lucknow style while embracing evolving expressions, Nishtha brings Kathak to diverse audiences through solo, duet, and group presentations. Beyond the stage, she conducts workshops, lecture-demonstrations, and mentorship programs, nurturing the next generation of dancers while sharing Kathak’s history, technique, and aesthetics.

For Nishtha, Kathak is not just performance—it is storytelling, rhythm, and devotion, an artistic journey that bridges heritage and contemporary sensibility.

Repertoire

She has graced prestigious cultural and government platforms, from G20 events, the Red Fort, Doordarshan and Rajpath to international festivals and curated showcases across continents. Collaborations with renowned artists such as Maitreyee Pahari, Rekha Mehra, and Vidushi Saswati Sen further highlight her versatility and stage presence. She has also had the honor of performing before eminent dignitaries including President Droupadi Murmu, Prime Minister Narendra Modi, Home Minister Amit Shah, Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath and Defence Minister Rajnath Singh, bringing the elegance of Kathak to India’s highest platforms.

My performances blend traditional Lucknow gharana compositions with innovative presentations that highlight rhythm, grace, and expressive storytelling. These collaborations and stage experiences have enriched my artistry, allowing me to bring diverse flavors of Kathak to audiences worldwide.
